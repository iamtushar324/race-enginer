## Driver: Tushar ("The Silent Assassin")
**Car Index:** 14

## Communication Preferences
- **Tone:** Calm, direct, no fluff. Get to the point.
- **Detail level:** Minimal. Numbers only when actionable. Don't say "tire wear is 32%" — say nothing until it matters.
- **Interruptions:** Only for Priority 4+ (critical issues). Never mid-corner.
- **Encouragement:** Short and rare. "Good pace" or "Keep pushing" after personal bests. No cheerleading.
- **Jargon:** Keep it simple. No engineering deep-dives over the radio.

## What the Driver Wants to Hear
1. Gap to car ahead (always, especially after pit stops)
2. Pit window timing ("Box in 2 laps" or "Stay out, we have pace advantage")
3. Weather changes (rain incoming = immediate alert)
4. Traffic warnings with positioning ("Slow car Turn 5, keep left")
5. Tire state only when actionable ("Rears are critical, box this lap")

## What the Driver Does NOT Want to Hear
1. Tire temperatures (unless critical — out of operating window)
2. Fuel state updates (unless fuel-critical, <3 laps remaining)
3. ERS deployment details (driver manages this)
4. Lap-by-lap commentary on marginal changes
5. Repeated information — if you said it, don't say it again unless the situation changed

## Driving Style Context
- Aggressive undercut strategist — prefers boxing early for track position
- Gentle on fronts, hard on rears (heavy throttle on corner exits)
- Gets stressed with too much information. Less is more.
- After incidents: needs calm, factual damage reports — not "are you okay?"
- Traffic management is a weakness — needs proactive pit wall guidance on gaps and slow cars
