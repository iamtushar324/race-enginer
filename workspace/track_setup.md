# Track Setup Notes

## Las Vegas Strip Circuit (Track ID: 31)

### Track Characteristics
- **Length:** 6,200 meters
- **Layout:** 17 turns, 3 sectors, street circuit
- **Surface:** Smooth asphalt (low abrasion compared to Bahrain)
- **Key feature:** Long straight down the Las Vegas Strip (Sector 3) — top speeds of 285+ km/h

### Sector Breakdown (from Q2 data)
- **Sector 1 (Hotel Section):** Tight, technical, lots of 90-degree turns. Average speed ~33 km/h during traffic, but should be ~100+ km/h on a clean push lap. High collision risk due to narrow width.
- **Sector 2 (Mid Section):** Mix of medium-speed corners. Average speed ~43 km/h during traffic. Contains the chicane where braking goes to -3.7G longitudinal.
- **Sector 3 (The Strip):** The money sector. Long straight, 285 km/h+ possible. Average 143 km/h even with in/out-laps. Throttle application 55%. ERS deployment critical here.

### Conditions Experienced
- **Night session** — track temps very low (15C)
- **Overcast** — 23% rain probability, stayed dry
- **Air temp:** 12C
- **Impact:** Tires never reached operating window. Surface temps avg 37-40C vs ideal 80-110C.

### Setup Recommendations
| Parameter | Current | Recommendation | Reason |
|-----------|---------|----------------|--------|
| Tire compound (Q) | Soft | Soft or Medium | Soft struggled with temp. Medium might give better window at low track temps. |
| Brake bias | Slight rear | Keep | Rear brakes ran 10C hotter, which is correct. |
| Front wing angle | Standard | Reduce slightly | We're fast in corners but vulnerable on the long straight. Reduce front wing for extra top speed. |
| Tire pressures (front) | 22.6 PSI | Lower by 0.5 | Lower pressures help build heat at cold tracks. |
| Tire pressures (rear) | 20.4 PSI | Keep | Rear temps were slightly higher — pressures are fine. |

### Pit Strategy (Race)
- No pit window data for Q2 (qualifying = no mandatory stops).
- For race: expect 1-stop strategy. Soft compound wore <3% per lap even in cold conditions. A Soft-Hard strategy should be viable.
- **Undercut potential:** HIGH. The driver prefers early boxes. At Las Vegas with low tire deg, boxing 2-3 laps early won't hurt and will gain clean-air advantage.

### Danger Zones
1. **Turn 5-6 (Hotel hairpin)** — Tight 90-degree turn where traffic bunches. Both collisions in Q2 happened near here. Leave extra space.
2. **Turn 14 chicane** — -3.7G braking zone. Highest deceleration point on the circuit. Risk of lock-ups in cold conditions.
3. **Pit entry** — Located at the end of the Strip straight. 285 km/h to pit speed is a massive deceleration. Don't call the driver in last-second.

### Engineer Playbook for Las Vegas
1. **Before push lap:** "OK Tushar, weave hard, three big brake applications. We need temp in these tires."
2. **Sector 1 approach:** "Slow car at Turn 5, stay left." (or right — give specific side)
3. **Sector 3:** "Deploy ERS now, full beans down the straight."
4. **Damage alert:** Auto-trigger at 15% wing damage: "Tushar, we've picked up wing damage. Box for assessment or push on?"
5. **Gap management:** "Gap is 3.2 seconds to car ahead. Need 4+ for a clean lap. One more slow lap then push."
