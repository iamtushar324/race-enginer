# Past Race Data & Historical Insights

## Bahrain (Pre-Season Testing)
- The track surface here is extremely abrasive.
- Rear tires overheat rapidly in Sector 2 (the twisty section).
- **CRITICAL THRESHOLD:** If rear tire wear exceeds 45%, lap times will drop by 1.5 seconds. You MUST recommend an immediate pit stop if rear wear hits 45% or higher.

## Setup Learnings
- We are running higher downforce than the rest of the grid. This means we are fast in corners but vulnerable on straights.
- Tell the driver to deploy ERS (Energy Recovery System) early on the main straights to defend.

## Las Vegas Q2 (2026-03-01) — Session Debrief

### Session Summary
- **Track:** Las Vegas Strip Circuit (ID 31), 6200m
- **Session:** Q2 (Qualifying 2)
- **Duration:** ~36 minutes (20:19 to 20:55)
- **Weather:** Overcast throughout, 12C air / 15C track, 23% rain probability (stayed dry)
- **Safety Car:** None deployed
- **Compound:** Soft (C5)
- **Result:** Retired at 20:48:44, then flashbacked and continued. FR wing destroyed to 100%.

### Tire Performance
- **Wear was minimal** — max wear across session: FL 2.60%, FR 2.75%, RL 2.72%, RR 2.91%.
- RR wore fastest (confirms driver profile: heavy throttle on exits pushes the rears).
- Soft compound was correct for Q2 at Las Vegas. Tire deg is not an issue on a single push lap.
- **Tire temps stayed cold** — Surface avg 37-40C (ideal is 80-110C). Track temp of 15C made it very hard to get heat into the tires. This is a night session issue at Las Vegas.
- **Inner temps also cold** — avg 51-52C vs ideal 85-105C. The tires were never in their operating window.
- **LEARNING:** At Las Vegas night sessions with track temps below 20C, run extra formation laps or weave aggressively on out-laps to build tire temperature. Consider starting on Mediums for better thermal window.

### Brake Performance
- Brake temps: avg 178-192C, max 493-509C. Well within operating range (ideal 200-900C). Brakes were on the cold side — consistent with not pushing hard enough on push laps.
- RL/RR brakes ran ~10C hotter than fronts — normal rear-biased brake balance.

### Tire Pressures
- Front pressures: 22.6 PSI avg (range 21.4-24.5)
- Rear pressures: 20.4 PSI avg (range 19.4-22.0)
- ~2.2 PSI difference front-to-rear. Standard setup. Pressures were stable.

### ERS & Fuel
- ERS stayed between 94-100% all session. Deployed in Hotlap mode 91% of the time. Efficient harvesting — MGUH harvested 210kJ, MGUK 46kJ per lap.
- Fuel: 5.6-6.3kg in tank, 2.7-3.1 laps remaining. Adequate for Q2 runs.
- **LEARNING:** ERS management was good. No intervention needed.

### Damage Report
- **FR wing: DESTROYED (100%)**. Escalation timeline:
  1. 20:26:27 — Light contact, FR wing 16% damaged
  2. 20:49:47 — Flashback repair cleared damage to 0%
  3. 20:50:25 — Heavy contact, 0% → 70% → 87% in seconds
  4. 20:51:24 — Worsened to 100%
- All other damage: 0%. No engine, gearbox, floor, or diffuser damage.
- **LEARNING:** The FR wing is the most exposed component at Las Vegas (lots of close-quarters racing in the hotel section). Warn driver to leave more space on right side when passing.

### Incident Analysis
- **Retirement (20:48:44):** Car was stationary, then used flashback. Likely a terminal collision or wall contact. Full brake lock at 107km/h with steering input suggests emergency avoidance.
- **Collision 1 (20:54:54):** Sector 1, speed dropped 108→31km/h. Heavy steering inputs during recovery (-0.90 to -0.57). Contact with slower traffic.
- **Collision 2 (20:55:14):** Sector 2, speed dropped 113→52km/h then recovered. Another traffic incident.
- **LEARNING:** All incidents were in traffic. At Las Vegas, the circuit has narrow sections where passing is risky. Engineer must give 3-second advance warnings about slow cars, especially through the hotel section (Sector 1).

### G-Force Analysis
- Max lateral: 3.61G (high cornering forces through the sweeping turns)
- Max longitudinal: -3.70G (hard braking into Turn 14 chicane)
- Max positive longitudinal: 1.39G (acceleration out of slow corners)
- **LEARNING:** The car generates extreme lateral load — 3.61G is near the limit. This explains the vulnerability to contact (small nudge at high lateral G = instant spin/damage).

### Key Race Engineer Actions Needed
1. **Temperature management** — Call "weave and brake hard on out-lap" to get tire temps up.
2. **Traffic alerts** — Provide 3-second warnings: "Slow car ahead Turn 8" style calls.
3. **Gap management from pit** — Build a 4+ second gap before sending the driver on a push lap. The Q2 traffic was chaotic.
4. **Wing damage monitoring** — Set an auto-alert at 15% FR wing damage. At Las Vegas, even minor contact escalates fast.
